# BARS Pilot Client Guide

In the real world, aerodrome controllers use stopbars as a barrier of illuminated lights preventing aircraft from entering active runways. Within Microsoft Flight Simulator, this can be emulated using the BARS client. Stopbar state is shared between pilots and controllers participating in the plugin, creating an immersive and realistic ground operations experience.

<Warning>
  Due to simulator limitations in MSFS 2020, the stopbar lights may not render
  until closer to the holding point. However, this does not affect the stopbar's
  functionality and will operate as intended. Additionally, **it is
  recommended** to enable the bloom setting, as without it, the visual effects
  of the stopbars will not be accurate.
</Warning>

## What are Stopbars?

Stopbars are safety systems used at airports to prevent runway incursions by providing pilots with clear visual guidance about when it's safe to proceed. When active (red), they signal pilots to hold their position. When inactive (unilluminated or green, depending on your settings), they indicate clearance to proceed.
