## Configuring Your Airport Scenery Packages

Once the Pilot Client is installed, you'll need to configure which scenery package you use for each airport.

### Finding Your Airport

Navigate to the **Airport List** section in the Pilot Client. You can search for airports by:
- **ICAO code** (e.g., `EGLL`)
- **Package name** (e.g., `FlyTampa`)
- **Airport name** (e.g., `East Midlands`)

### Selecting Your Scenery Package

<Steps>
<Step title="Find your desired airport">
  Use the search function to locate your airport.
</Step>

<Step title="Click the dropdown menu">
  Click the **dropdown menu** next to the airport ICAO code.
</Step>

<Step title="Choose a scenery package">
  Choose from the available scenery packages for that airport.
</Step>

<Step title="Verify your selection">
  Your selection will update where lights are placed in your simulator.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
If an airport does not appear in the list or a specific scenery package is missing, it may not have been contributed yet. You can contribute to BARS by following the [scenery contribution guide](https://preview.stopbars.com/contribute).
</Note>

## System Tray and Window Management

### Minimize to System Tray

The Pilot Client can be minimized to your system tray for quick access while keeping your desktop clean. Click the **minimize button** in the top-right corner of the window to minimize the application to your system tray.

![Minimize button](/images/v2/minimize-button.png)

### System Tray Icon Settings

Right-click on the BARS Pilot Client icon in your system tray to access additional settings:

![System tray context menu](/images/v2/system-tray-context-menu.png)

#### Auto Minimize on Start

Enabling **Auto Minimize on Start** will automatically minimize the Pilot Client to your system tray when you launch it. 

<Tip>
We recommend enabling this setting after you've configured your scenery packages, so the application starts quietly in the background without taking up screen space.
</Tip>

#### Discord Rich Presence

Toggle **Discord Rich Presence** to show your activity in the BARS Pilot Client on your Discord profile. When enabled, your Discord status will display:
- Your nearest airport (automatically detected from your simulator position)
- Your current server connection status (Connected, Disconnected, Error, or No VATSIM Connection)
- Your active connected flight simulator (MSFS 2020 or MSFS 2024) with a small icon indicator

This allows your Discord friends to see what you're up to in the simulator!

![Discord Rich Presence example](/images/v2/discord-rich-presence-example.png)

<Tip>
The nearest airport is automatically resolved based on your current aircraft position in the simulator when available.
</Tip>

## Auto-Start Configuration

### Starting Pilot Client with Your Flight Simulator

The BARS Pilot Client is configured to **automatically launch when you start your flight simulator by default**. This ensures BARS lights are active whenever you fly without requiring manual startup.

If you need to disable auto-start or re-enable it, you can modify this setting at any time:

<Steps>
<Step title="Open the BARS Installer">
  Launch the **BARS Installer** application.
</Step>

<Step title="Navigate to Settings">
  Go to the **Settings** page.
</Step>

<Step title="Configure auto-start option">
  Check or uncheck the **"Start Pilot Client automatically with Microsoft Flight Simulator"** option as desired.
</Step>
</Steps>

The Pilot Client will automatically launch (or not launch) whenever you start Microsoft Flight Simulator 2020 or 2024, depending on your preference.

<Tip>
Combined with the **Auto Minimize on Start** setting (see above), the Pilot Client will quietly launch in the background whenever you start your simulator, keeping your desktop clean while ensuring BARS lights are active.
</Tip>

<Note>
For detailed information about the installer and its configuration options, see the [Installation guide](/pilot-client/installation).
</Note>
