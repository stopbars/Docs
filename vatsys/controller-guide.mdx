# Controller Operations

This guide covers the day-to-day operations of the BARS system, from basic stop bar control to advanced multi-controller scenarios.

## Stop Bar Fundamentals

### Understanding Stop Bar States

Stop bars have two primary states that controllers can manage:

<Tabs>
<Tab title="Active (Red)">
  **Stop bars are illuminated red**
  - Aircraft must stop and hold at the stop bar
  - No movement past the stop bar is permitted
  - Default state for all runway intersections
</Tab>

<Tab title="Inactive (Off)">
  **Stop bars are turned off/dark**
  - Aircraft may proceed past the stop bar location
  - Used when clearance has been issued to enter/cross runway
  - Automatically reactivates after 45 seconds
</Tab>
</Tabs>

<Info>
  In the real world, stop bars use red lights to signal "stop" and turn off
  completely when aircraft are cleared to proceed. Green stop bars in BARS
  indicate runways controlled by other controllers or unclaimed runways.
</Info>

## Basic Stop Bar Operations

### Standard Operating Procedure

<Steps>
<Step title="Issue Clearance">
  Provide standard phraseology for runway entry or crossing:
  
  ```
  "QFA123, runway 34L, line up and wait"
  "UAL456, cross runway 34L, contact ground 121.9"
  ```
</Step>

<Step title="Deactivate Stop Bar">
  Click on the appropriate stop bar to turn it off, allowing the aircraft to
  proceed.
  <Frame>
    <img
      src="/images/stopbar-operation.gif"
      alt="Animation showing stop bar being clicked to deactivate"
    />
  </Frame>
</Step>

<Step title="Monitor Aircraft Movement">
  Ensure the aircraft proceeds as cleared and monitor for compliance.
</Step>

<Step title="Reactivate Stop Bar">
  Click the stop bar again once the aircraft has cleared the protected area.
  
  <Tip>
  If you forget to reactivate the stop bar, it will automatically turn back on after 45 seconds for safety.
  </Tip>
</Step>
</Steps>

### Stop Bar Locations

Stop bars are positioned at critical runway intersection points:

- **Runway entry points** from taxiways
- **Runway crossing points** for intersecting taxiways
- **Hold short lines** at runway thresholds
- **Intermediate stop positions** on complex taxiway systems

## Advanced Operations

### Runway Crossing Coordination

When aircraft need to cross active runways, coordination between controllers is essential:

<Steps>
<Step title="Coordinate with Tower">
  Complete standard coordination for runway crossing:
  
  ```
  Ground: "Tower, cross runway 34L, QFA123"
  Tower: "QFA123 approved to cross 34L, contact ground"
  ```
</Step>

<Step title="Issue Crossing Clearance">
  Provide clearance to the aircraft: ``` "QFA123, cross runway 34L, contact
  tower 120.5" ```
</Step>

<Step title="Manage Stop Bar">
  As the ADC controller, deactivate the stop bar at the crossing point and reactivate once the aircraft has crossed completely.
</Step>
</Steps>

### Multiple Aircraft Management

When managing several aircraft simultaneously:

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="Sequential Runway Entries">
  For multiple aircraft lining up on the same runway:
  
  1. Clear first aircraft and deactivate stop bar
  2. Wait for aircraft to clear the stop bar area
  3. Reactivate stop bar before clearing next aircraft
  4. Repeat process for subsequent aircraft
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Simultaneous Operations">
  For operations at different runway intersections:
  
  - Each stop bar can be controlled independently
  - Multiple stop bars can be deactivated simultaneously
  - Monitor all active clearances to prevent conflicts
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Multi-Controller Operations

### Dual ADC Positions

At airports with multiple ADC positions, runway jurisdiction must be clearly established:

<Steps>
<Step title="Coordinate Runway Responsibilities">
  Determine which controller will manage which runways based on:
  - Standard operating procedures
  - Traffic flow requirements
  - Operational necessity
</Step>

<Step title="Claim Respective Runways">
  Each controller selects only the runways within their jurisdiction through the
  runway selection interface.
  <Frame>
    <img
      src="/images/dual-adc-runway-selection.png"
      alt="Runway selection showing some runways unavailable due to other controller claims"
    />
  </Frame>
</Step>

<Step title="Coordinate Boundary Operations">
  When aircraft operations affect multiple controllers' areas:
  - Complete standard coordination procedures
  - Ensure clear handoff points
  - Maintain awareness of adjacent runway activities
</Step>
</Steps>

### Jurisdiction Management

<Warning>
  Once a runway is claimed by one controller, other controllers cannot select
  it. Coordination is required to transfer runway control between positions.
</Warning>

**Best Practices for Multi-Controller Operations:**

- Establish clear runway assignments before operations begin
- Use standard coordination procedures for boundary crossings
- Maintain regular communication during complex operations
- Release runway claims when going offline or transferring positions

## Centre Controller Operations

C1/C3 controllers can manage stop bars at remote airports using a simplified interface:

### Remote Airport Control

<Steps>
<Step title="Access BARS Interface">
  From your centre position, navigate to **Windows > BARS** in vatSys.
</Step>

<Step title="Enter Airport ICAO">
  Input the four-letter ICAO code for the airport you wish to control.
  <Frame>
    <img
      src="/images/centre-icao-entry.png"
      alt="ICAO code entry dialog for centre controllers"
    />
  </Frame>
</Step>

<Step title="Control Stop Bars">
  Once connected, operate stop bars using the same procedures as ADC controllers.
  
  <Info>
  Centre controllers can currently manage one airport at a time. Multi-airport support is planned for future releases.
  </Info>
</Step>
</Steps>

### Centre Controller Limitations

Current limitations for centre controller operations:

- **Single airport control**: Only one airport window supported per session
- **No runway claiming**: All runways at the selected airport are available
- **Coordination required**: Must coordinate with local controllers if present

## Emergency Procedures

### System Failures

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="BARS System Unavailable">
  If the BARS system becomes unavailable:
  
  1. Inform pilots that stop bar indications may not be available
  2. Use standard phraseology emphasizing hold short requirements
  3. Provide additional positive confirmation for runway entries
  4. Report system issues through appropriate channels
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Individual Stop Bar Malfunction">
  For single stop bar failures:
  
  1. Treat the location as if stop bars are permanently active
  2. Use standard hold short procedures with extra vigilance
  3. Coordinate with ground crews if available for visual confirmation
  4. Report the specific stop bar issue to maintenance
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Backup Procedures

When BARS is unavailable, controllers should:

- Revert to standard hold short procedures
- Increase vigilance for runway incursions
- Use positive confirmation for all runway movements
- Coordinate more extensively with pilots regarding runway status

## Operational Tips

<Tip>
  **Timing Stop Bar Operations** - Deactivate stop bars just before aircraft
  reach them - Don't deactivate too early to maintain safety buffer - Reactivate
  immediately after aircraft clear the area
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Managing High Traffic Periods** - Plan stop bar operations in advance -
  Coordinate with adjacent controllers early - Use progressive taxi clearances
  to manage flow - Monitor for aircraft compliance continuously
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Communication Best Practices** - Use standard phraseology for all runway
  operations - Confirm aircraft acknowledge runway clearances - Provide
  progressive updates during complex movements - Coordinate proactively with
  other controller positions
</Tip>

## Common Issues and Solutions

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="Aircraft not stopping at stop bars">
  **Possible causes:**
  - Pilot not using BARS client
  - Network synchronization delay
  - Aircraft simulation limitations
  
  **Solutions:**
  - Query pilot about BARS client usage
  - Use voice instructions as backup
  - Report persistent issues to BARS support
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Stop bars not responding to clicks">
  **Possible causes:** - Network connectivity issues - Server synchronization
  problems - Interface focus problems **Solutions:** - Check connection status
  in control bar - Try clicking directly on the stop bar indicator - Restart
  BARS plugin if necessary
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Runway selection not working">
  **Possible causes:**
  - Another controller has claimed the runway
  - Airport not properly claimed
  - Network communication failure
  
  **Solutions:**
  - Coordinate with other controllers
  - Re-claim airport if necessary
  - Check BARS server status
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Performance Standards

Controllers using BARS should maintain:

- **Response time**: Stop bars should be managed within 2-3 seconds of issuing clearances
- **Accuracy**: All runway movements should be properly coordinated with stop bar status
- **Communication**: Clear and timely coordination with other controllers and pilots
- **Safety**: No compromise to safety standards while using the system
